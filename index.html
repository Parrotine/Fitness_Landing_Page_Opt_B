Strength and Balance

Sitting shuts off the electrical activity in your muscles. When standing, you engage more muscles in your legs and core, improving your strength, balance, and overall fitness.

Metabolism and Calorie Burn

Sitting turns off fat-burning enzymes and slows metabolism to a crawl. Standing up reverses this process and burns more calories. In fact, by standing instead of sitting for 4 hours a day in a 5-day work week, you burn enough extra calories to equal a 10k run.

For every hour you spend sitting, you can lose up to 16% of the benefits you gain from a workout. At a sit/stand desk, you're more likely to stand up, move around, and maintain more of the benefits of exercise.
